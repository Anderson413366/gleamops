# Manager and Supervisor Training Handbook

> Use this guide to train frontline leaders on how to run the business in GleamOps.
> Focused on managers and supervisors, with simple routines and role-based checklists.

---

## What This Handbook Covers

This handbook teaches managers and supervisors:

- where they work in the app
- what they are responsible for
- how to move from planning to execution
- what to review daily, weekly, and monthly
- what good operating discipline looks like

---

## The Two Leadership Roles

| Role | Primary focus | Main modules |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Manager | planning, coordination, exception handling, reporting | Home, Staff Schedule, Dispatch, Work Orders, Field Tools, Workforce, Time & Pay, Reports |
| Supervisor | day-of team oversight and field execution | Home, Dispatch, Work Orders, Field Tools, Shifts & Time, Compliance |

Simple rule:

- Managers shape the plan.
- Supervisors run the day.

---

## The Core Mental Model

Use this sequence every day:

1. Review the plan
2. confirm labor coverage
3. confirm work readiness
4. run execution
5. capture what happened
6. close exceptions

In GleamOps, that usually means:

`Home -> Staff Schedule -> Dispatch -> Work Orders -> Field Tools / Shifts & Time -> Compliance / Time & Pay`

## Daily Manager Routine

### Before the day starts

1. Open **Home** and review the KPI cards and alerts.
2. Open **Staff Schedule** to review coverage gaps and future risk.
3. Open **Dispatch** to review today's board.
4. Check **Work Orders** for open or aging items.
5. Check **Field Tools** or **Time Alerts** for anything unresolved from the previous day.

### During the day

1. Monitor **Dispatch** for changes and exceptions.
2. Review **Work Orders** for stuck or delayed work.
3. Review **Shifts & Time** for labor capture issues.
4. Escalate quality or incident issues into **Compliance**.

### End of day

1. Confirm major open work has a next step.
2. Confirm route / shift execution is not left hanging.
3. Confirm unresolved exceptions are visible for tomorrow.

---

## Daily Supervisor Routine

### Before the shift

1. Open **Dispatch**.
2. Confirm team assignments.
3. Review **Work Orders** for site-specific expectations.
4. Review **Field Tools** checklists and requests.

### During the shift

1. Confirm staff are working the correct assignments.
2. Resolve issues using **Dispatch**, **Work Orders**, and **Field Tools**.
3. Make sure staff use **Shifts & Time** correctly.
4. Review quality checks and incidents in **Compliance** when needed.

### End of shift

1. Confirm work statuses are accurate.
2. Confirm unresolved issues are logged.
3. Confirm time capture is complete.

---

## Weekly Manager Routine

1. Build or review the upcoming schedule in **Staff Schedule**.
2. Review leave and availability impacts.
3. Review recurring open issues in **Work Orders**.
4. Review timesheet and attendance quality in **Time & Pay**.
5. Review team readiness in **Workforce** and **Compliance**.
6. Review summary performance in **Reports**.

---

## Weekly Supervisor Routine

1. Review recurring field issues and quality misses.
2. Review route execution pain points.
3. Review checklist compliance and inspection follow-through.
4. Escalate staffing or training concerns to managers.

---

## Monthly Leadership Routine

Managers should review:

- schedule quality and recurring coverage risk
- labor accuracy
- open work trend
- quality trend
- inventory and readiness friction
- reporting trend

Supervisors should review:

- repeated site issues
- repeated team performance gaps
- training needs
- incident patterns

## Red Flags Managers Should Catch Early

- coverage gaps that keep reappearing
- visible work without clear assignees
- open tickets with no recent movement
- field requests that sit too long
- poor time capture discipline
- inspections failing in repeating patterns

---

## Red Flags Supervisors Should Catch Early

- staff unsure where to go or what to do
- route context missing from the board
- work marked complete without evidence of execution
- staff not clocking correctly
- checklist or inspection steps skipped

---

## Escalation Rules

Escalate to a manager when:

- schedule needs structural change
- staffing gap cannot be solved in the field
- repeated quality issues appear
- time or labor exceptions affect payroll
- site/client issue has account-level impact

Escalate to owner/admin or department leadership when:

- system settings are wrong
- repeated cross-module failures occur
- reporting no longer reflects operational reality
- compliance risk is material

## Training Checklist

### A manager is ready when they can:

- build and review a schedule
- manage the dispatch board
- monitor open work
- resolve field exceptions
- review labor quality
- read operational reports

### A supervisor is ready when they can:

- interpret today's board
- guide the team through assigned work
- resolve basic field issues
- ensure time capture is clean
- escalate quality or compliance issues correctly
